Energy Efficient Televisions

Choose the right TV to reduce your energy consumption without compromising on entertainment quality.

TV Energy Consumption in Australia

The average Australian household has 2.2 televisions, consuming approximately 200-400 kWh annually per TV. With rising electricity costs, choosing an energy-efficient model can save you $50-100 per year per television.

Energy Consumption by TV Type

LED/LCD TVs

Power Usage: 60-120W

Annual Cost: $40-80

Most energy-efficient option with excellent picture quality.

OLED TVs

Power Usage: 100-150W

Annual Cost: $65-100

Superior picture quality with moderate energy consumption.

Plasma TVs (Legacy)

Power Usage: 200-400W

Annual Cost: $130-260

High energy consumption - consider upgrading to newer technology.

Tips for Reducing TV Energy Consumption

  • Use automatic brightness settings to adjust to room lighting
  • Enable power-saving modes when available
  • Turn off TVs completely rather than leaving on standby
  • Consider smaller screen sizes for secondary rooms
  • Look for Energy Star certified models
